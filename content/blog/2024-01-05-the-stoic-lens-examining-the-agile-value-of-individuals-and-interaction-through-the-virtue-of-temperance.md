The Stoic Virtue of Temperance: A Balancing Act

In essence, temperance is about finding a harmonious balance. It is among the four cardinal virtues of Stoicism, along with wisdom, courage, and justice. For Stoics, temperance isn't just about restraining from physical pleasures. It's a broader concept, addressing how we manage our desires and impulses and consciously try to find equilibrium in our actions, thoughts, and feelings.

Think of it as an inner compass directing us away from extremes. When we practice temperance, we seek the middle ground, avoiding the pitfalls of overindulgence and deprivation.

The Role of Temperance in Individual Interactions

When it comes to individual interactions, temperance plays a critical role. It helps us establish and maintain a measure of respectful, constructive discourse. Stoics believed in the importance of deep, meaningful connections with others, and temperance fosters this by encouraging us to communicate effectively and empathetically.

Practising temperance can mean actively listening to another person's viewpoints without interrupting or being swayed by our biases. It can mean speaking up but doing so thoughtfully and without aggression. It prompts us to be aware of the impact of our words and actions on others.

Character is fate.

- Heraclitus

The quote above encapsulates the Stoic belief that our character, shaped by virtues like temperance, determines our life's course. In the context of Agile, this suggests that a team's success is profoundly affected by its members' individual temperaments and interactive habits.

Temperance and Agile Value

Agile puts a high premium on individuals and interactions. It prioritises human-centric collaboration over processes and tools. It's not impressive coding skills or cutting-edge software that makes an Agile team successful. Instead, it's the individuals - their skills, interactions, and ability to work together towards a common goal.

Here, the virtue of temperance can make a significant difference. A temperate team is more likely to navigate the ebbs and flows of product development successfully. They can maintain a balanced perspective, even when faced with stress, time pressures, and ever-changing priorities. An Agile team can cultivate resilient, productive, and harmonious working relationships by practising temperance.
